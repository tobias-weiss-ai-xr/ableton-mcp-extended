## NEXT STEPS TO COMPLETE

### 1. Load Instruments
- **Dub Bass:** Sub-bass synth (Operator, Serum, Subtractor)
- **Drums:** Drum rack or individual drum sounds
- **Atmosphere:** Pad synth with reverb
- **Dub Melody:** Lead synth or pluck
- **FX:** Sampler with sweeps and impacts
- **Percussion:** Percussion sounds

### 2. Add Effects (Essential for Dub)
- **Reverb:** Heavy reverb on atmosphere, melody, FX
- **Delay:** Dub delays on bass, melody, drums (use sends)
- **Filters:** Low-pass filters for depth
- **Compression:** Glue drum elements together
- **Sidechain:** Duck bass against kick

### 3. Mixing
- **Bass:** Heavy compression, EQ (boost low end)
- **Kick:** Punchy, prominent
- **Snare:** Crisp, add room reverb
- **Atmosphere:** Wide stereo, low volume
- **Melody:** Add delay and reverb
- **FX:** Automate for dramatic effects

### 4. Automation
- Filter sweeps on buildups
- Reverb sends on drops
- Delay time changes for variation
- Volume automation for dynamics
- Pan automation for stereo width

### 5. Arrangement in Ableton
- Duplicate clips in Session View
- Arrange clips for each section
- Use Scene Launch to trigger sections
- Add transition clips between sections
- Automate effects throughout song
